The solar system consists of the Sun and the objects that orbit it, including eight planets, moons, dwarf planets, asteroids, and comets. It spans vast distances, with each planet having unique characteristics and conditions. The Sun's gravity keeps everything in the solar system in orbit, providing energy that supports life on Earth.

SUN

The Sun is a massive ball of hot gases, primarily hydrogen and helium, located at the center of our solar system. It provides the light and heat necessary for life on Earth and influences our planet’s climate and weather patterns. The Sun is about 4.6 billion years old and is expected to continue shining for another 5 billion years.

Its energy is produced through nuclear fusion in its core, where hydrogen atoms combine to form helium, releasing immense energy. The Sun also affects space weather, creating solar flares and solar winds that can impact satellites and communication systems on Earth.
